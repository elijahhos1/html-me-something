The Years Before LaunchCode

2016-17: Truman State University

During my freshman year at Truman State, I studied computer science and found myself very intrigued by the subject.

While the coursework was very demanding and a new concept for me, every program I completed gave me a sense of accomplishment.

Fall 2017: University of Missouri Columbia

As it turns out, I was quite indecisive about where I wanted to study computer science.

Since Truman State was a little boring for me (it was set in a town of 7,000 people after all), I decided to switch universities.

At Mizzou I learned C. While it was a hard language to work in, I came to appreciate how powerful it was. Learning about pointers and how to use them was especially interesting to me, as I got to learn about how programs store variables in memory and how to manipulate that memory. A difficult concept at first but also very rewarding and informative.

However, after my time at Mizzou I no longer pursued a computer science degree. I believe that at the time, I didn't have the work ethic to complete my "gen-ed" coursework, and I wasn't in the right headspace to continue my degree.

After College: 2017-2020

During the next few years, I made a few attempts to go back to school but couldn't find the motivation to complete most of my work. I felt that the community college courses I was taking didn't have the same feel of the universities I attended.

Around this time, I mostly worked for my stepdad doing landscaping in the hell that is Missouri in the summer. I knew that working for him was not what I wanted to do with my life, but I had no direction and no idea what to do next.

Eventually my mother told me that one of her coworkers was currently doing a program called LaunchCode. I had never heard of it before, though I knew coding bootcamps existed and hadn't taken the time to research them. After she graduated from the program and quickly got a job, I thought it may be what I needed.

I believe that LaunchCode really is right for me, as I always wanted to pursue computer science and computer science alone. It has been something that I'm relatively good at, and to pursue this profession without having to pay a stupid amount of money is a godsend.

So here's hoping that it'll all turn out well in the end!
